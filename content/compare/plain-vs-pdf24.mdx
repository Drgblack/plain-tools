## Scenario scorecard

Use this scorecard with your own team context.

| Small team with mixed technical confidence | Strong | Medium | Route consistency is key |
| Desktop-managed environment with central controls | Medium to strong | Strong | Existing deployment patterns matter |
| Quick one-off task under deadline pressure | Strong | Medium | Simpler route choices reduce mistakes |

## Route clarity in daily use

Route clarity means users immediately understand whether they are on a local path or a remote path. If that is ambiguous, teams drift.

A route that is easy to recognise is easier to enforce.

## Verification workload

Evaluate how many steps are required for a reviewer to confirm behaviour.

Lower workload usually means:

- faster onboarding
- fewer policy exceptions
- better repeatability of trust checks

## 15-minute pilot method

1. Select one representative workflow (for example, merge and compress).
2. Run the same sample files through both candidates.
3. Capture request behaviour and offline continuity notes.
4. Ask a second team member to repeat the test independently.

If two reviewers reach the same conclusion quickly, confidence is higher.

## Decision guidance

Choose the option that your team can execute correctly under normal time pressure, not just in ideal test conditions.

For privacy-sensitive handling, predictable route behaviour and quick verification usually outperform raw feature breadth.

## Practical verdict

Plain tends to fit teams that want a clear local-first route with low verification overhead. PDF24 can fit well where route choice is already governed and consistently enforced. The right decision depends on operational discipline as much as technology.
